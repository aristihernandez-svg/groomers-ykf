XXXXXX is a mobile operations platform built specifically for the ground crew team at Skycare's Kitchener–Waterloo base. It replaces paper checklists, group texts, and verbal handoffs with a single app every team member runs on their phone — no installation, no accounts, no IT department required.

Built in-house and deployed directly on the floor, it is live and used by the Skycare team every shift.

03 — Context

The Problem It Solves

Ground operations at a regional airport involve multiple roles, constant staff movement, and no margin for missed steps. Before XXXXXX, coordination at Skycare depended on informal tools — group texts, printed checklists, and tribal knowledge passed between shifts.

01
High turnover, no loss of institutional knowledge
Skycare groomers turn over every 6 to 12 months. XXXXXX embeds procedures into the platform — a new hire on day one follows the same process as a veteran, because the steps live in the app, not in someone's memory.
02
Consistent work quality across the team
Every groomer sees the same task lists, the same procedures, the same checklists — regardless of experience level. The quality of a shift no longer depends on who's working it.
03
A foundation for multi-base consistency
Because the platform is cloud-based and configurable, the same processes used at CYKF can be replicated at any Skycare base — creating company-wide operational standards without rebuilding anything from scratch.
04
A live management layer, not just a task tool
XXXXXX gives Skycare supervisors and management a live operational picture — task completion, fleet positions, vehicle status, supply levels, shift handoffs — all in one place, updated in real time. The team executes; the platform reports.

05 — Roadmap

What's Next

Four improvements planned for the near term — each building directly on what's already in use by Skycare. Click any item to read more.

01
Pilot / MX Snag Reference Log
Design complete ▾
A digital reference log for recurring aircraft observations — pilots and mechanics can note issues from their phone with a photo and description, organized by tail number. This is a planning and awareness tool for Skycare operations only. It does not replace and will never replace official logbook snag entries, which remain the regulatory record.
02
NFC Tags on Crew Cars
Up next ▾
Small stickers on each Skycare vehicle — tap with any phone and the car update form opens instantly. Faster than scanning a QR code, and no camera required. A two-second tap replaces a five-minute conversation at shift handoff.
03
Individual Secure Login
Planned ▾
Replace the current shared PIN with individual accounts — email or Google login. Each Skycare team member gets access only to what their role requires: groomers see the groomer view, supervisors see everything. This also creates a clean activity log per person for accountability and shift reporting.
04
App Store Listing
Planned ▾
Publish XXXXXX to the Apple App Store and Google Play. The app already works like a native app — this step packages it formally so it appears in the store, enables proper push notification prompts (higher acceptance rate than browser prompts), and ensures automatic updates reach every Skycare team member's phone without any action on their part.
