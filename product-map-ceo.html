Overview

What Is Skycare

Skycare is a mobile operations platform built specifically for the ground crew team at Kitchener Waterloo Regional Airport. It replaces paper checklists, group texts, and verbal handoffs with a single app every team member runs on their phone — no installation, no accounts, no IT department required.

Built in-house and deployed directly on the floor, it is live and used by the team every day.

Context

The Problem It Solves

Ground operations at a regional airport involve multiple roles, constant staff movement, and no margin for missed steps. Before Skycare, coordination depended on informal tools — group texts, printed checklists, and tribal knowledge passed between shifts.

1
High turnover, no loss of institutional knowledge

Groomers turn over every 6 to 12 months. Skycare embeds the procedures into the platform — a new hire on day one follows the same process as someone who has been here for years, because the steps live in the app, not in someone's memory.

2
Consistent work quality across the team

Every groomer sees the same task lists, the same procedures, the same checklists — regardless of experience level. The quality of a shift no longer depends on who's working it.

3
A foundation for multi-base consistency

Because the platform is cloud-based and configurable, the same processes used at CYKF can be replicated at any Skycare base — creating company-wide operational standards without rebuilding anything from scratch.

4
A live management layer, not just a task tool

Skycare is not just about cleaning planes. It gives supervisors and management a live operational picture — task completion, fleet positions, vehicle status, supply levels, shift handoffs — all in one place, updated in real time. The team executes; the platform reports. Management stays informed without having to ask.

Technology

How It Works

Built to be simple to maintain and cost nothing to run. Every team member opens the app in their phone browser — no app store, no account creation. Data lives in Google's Firebase, so all phones see the same information in real time.

💰 Infrastructure cost
  • Hosting — GitHub Pages (free)
  • Database — Firebase Firestore (free tier)
  • Notifications backend — GitHub Actions (free on public repos)
  • Scheduled jobs — cron-job.org (free)
  • Total: $0/month
📱 How team members access it
  • Open the URL on any phone browser
  • Enter the team PIN once
  • Install to home screen — looks and works like a native app
  • Works offline on the ramp — no connectivity required
Roadmap

What's Next

Four improvements planned for the near term, each building directly on what's already in use.

1
Pilot / MX Snag Reporting
Pilots and mechanics report aircraft defects directly from their phone — photo, description, severity. MX gets notified within minutes. Full snag history per aircraft, accessible from the cockpit via a QR sticker.
2
NFC Tags on Crew Cars
Small stickers on each vehicle — tap with any phone and the car update form opens instantly. Faster than scanning a QR code, and no camera required.
3
Secure Login
Replace the shared PIN with individual accounts (email or Google login). Allows per-role access — groomers see the groomer view, mechanics see the MX view, supervisors see everything.
4
App Store Listing
Publish to the Apple App Store and Google Play. The app already works like a native app — this step packages it formally so it appears in the store, enables system-style notification prompts, and ensures automatic updates to every phone without any action from the team.
