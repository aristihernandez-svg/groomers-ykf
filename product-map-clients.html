The Problem

Ground crews run on group texts and printed sheets. That's not a system.

When coordination is informal, accountability disappears. Things get missed, new hires take months to get up to speed, and every shift starts with questions that should already have answers.

01
Tasks get missed
No shared checklist means no shared accountability. If it's not tracked, it doesn't always happen.
02
Turnover breaks everything
Groomer tenure averages 6–12 months. When someone leaves, their process knowledge leaves with them.
03
No inbound visibility
Knowing an aircraft is 30 minutes out changes how your crew prepares. Without it, you're always reacting.
04
Vehicle status is a guess
Fuel levels, mileage, service dates — without a shared log, every shift starts from zero.
